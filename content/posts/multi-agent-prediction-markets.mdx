Prediction markets only work when outcomes are clear. On platforms like Polymarket you can bet on almost anything. That flexibility is the appeal, but it also creates a real bottleneck. If people can bet on anything, someone has to judge anything.

In practice the hard part is not trading. It is resolving the market. Human resolution teams check sources, interpret wording, and handle edge cases. Some markets are simple. Others take hours and often lead to disputes. This limitation makes it difficult for prediction markets to scale to millions of custom questions.

## Why Agents Help

Instead of relying on a single oracle, you can use a group of agents that work together to resolve a market. Each agent reads evidence differently, forms its own belief, and proposes a resolution. They do not need to be complex. They only need to be somewhat independent.

When agents disagree, that disagreement becomes useful. It highlights ambiguity, exposes weak reasoning, and forces the system to justify its final choice. The goal is not to create a perfect oracle. The goal is to create a resolution process that is transparent, stable, and consistent.

## RL vs Simpler Agent Approaches

You can train agents with reinforcement learning, but it is not required. RL gives you a way to reward agents that make strong judgments, identify inconsistencies, or surface missing information. RL also allows agents to improve over time as they observe more markets and compare their decisions to human resolutions.

The tradeoff is that RL can become brittle if the reward design is flawed. It can also be expensive to train and maintain. Pure RL systems often look strong in controlled settings but fail in rare corner cases that matter to traders.

A more straightforward approach is to combine supervised training, rule based checks, retrieval systems, and lightweight agent debate. This avoids reward hacking and cuts down on engineering overhead. The tradeoff is that it may be less adaptive and might miss subtle patterns that RL could capture.

The most practical design is usually a hybrid. RL shapes incentives at a high level while structured components provide guardrails and interpretation.

## Why This Increases Market Flexibility

If judgment becomes automated, prediction markets can expand far beyond what human resolution teams can handle. You can support more complex questions, rely on niche data sources, use conditional logic, and resolve markets faster.

The intention is not to remove humans. It is to build a scalable system that remains understandable and trustworthy. A well designed agent system acts as a simulation of human judgment. It only needs to be consistent, explainable, and accurate enough for traders to rely on it.
